# The Perfect 1‑Day Kyoto Itinerary for First‑Timers

<!-- HERO_IMAGE: Dawn at Fushimi Inari torii. Wide banner. -->

## Overview

- Focus: Fushimi Inari at dawn → Higashiyama walk → Gion evening.
- Pace: ~18,000 steps; use trains/taxis for hops; avoid buses at peak.
- Map: <!-- MAP_EMBED: Google My Maps route with times + alt route in rain. -->

## Timeline

### 06:00 — Fushimi Inari Taisha (Shrine)
- Why: Empty torii gates and cool air.
- Do: Walk 20–40 minutes up to quiet sections; turn back when satisfied.
- Transit: JR Nara Line to Inari or Keihan to Fushimi-Inari.
- Photos: <!-- PHOTO: portrait torii tunnel; ALT: vermilion torii tunnel at dawn. -->
- Read more: [/sights/fushimi-inari](/sights/fushimi-inari)

### 08:00 — Breakfast near Inari or Downtown
- Options: Onigiri/coffee near stations or sit‑down café downtown.

### 09:00 — Kiyomizu‑dera → Sannenzaka → Yasaka Shrine
- Why: Classic wooden streets and views.
- Route: Bus/taxi to Kiyomizu; walk down through Sannenzaka/Ninenzaka.
- Etiquette: No tripods; keep right; avoid blocking doorways.
- Photos: <!-- PHOTO: Kiyomizu stage; ALT: wooden temple stage overlooking Kyoto. -->
- Guides: [/sights/kiyomizu-dera](/sights/kiyomizu-dera), [/areas/higashiyama](/areas/higashiyama)

### 12:00 — Lunch in Higashiyama or Nishiki Market
- Ideas: Soba, tempura, tofu; quick bites at Nishiki (mind etiquette).
- Read: [/sights/nishiki-market](/sights/nishiki-market)

### 14:00 — Optional North Loop (Kinkaku‑ji/Ryoan‑ji)
- If energy/time: taxi between; or skip to Arashiyama next visit.
- Guide: [/sights/kinkaku-ji](/sights/kinkaku-ji)

### 16:00 — Downtime / Café / Hotel Check‑in
- Short rest to reset before evening.

### 18:00 — Gion & Yasaka at Night
- Stroll lantern‑lit lanes; respect privacy; no chasing Maiko.
- Optional: Book our Gion Sake Walk for stories + tastings.
- Guide: [/areas/gion-guide](/areas/gion-guide), [/sights/yasaka-shrine](/sights/yasaka-shrine)

## Rain Plan

- Swap Inari hike with covered arcades/museums.
- See: [/itineraries/rain](/itineraries/rain) and [/rainy/covered-streets](/rainy/covered-streets)

## Transport Cheatsheet

- IC cards work across JR/Keihan/Hankyu; Google Maps or Navitime for routes.
- Buses: pay after; exact fare or IC; enter front/back depending on line.
- Taxis: tap credit or pay cash; keep receipt.

## Budget

- Entries: ~¥1,000–¥1,800; Lunch ¥1,200–¥2,000; Dinner ¥2,000–¥4,000; Transit ~¥900–¥1,200.

## FAQs

1) Can I squeeze in Arashiyama? — Not comfortably. Save it for Day 2.
2) Best spot for sunset? — Kiyomizu‑dera or Shogunzuka (taxi up, bus/taxi down).
3) Is Kyoto Station luggage storage safe? — Yes, use coin lockers or leave bags at hotel.
