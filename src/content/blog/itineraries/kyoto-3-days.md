# 3 Days in Kyoto: Complete First-Timer Itinerary

## Fast Facts

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Best for | First-timers who want to see the highlights well |
| Daily budget | ¥10,000–¥18,000/person (transport, entry, food) |
| Transit method | IC card + occasional taxi |
| Accommodation tip | Central or Gion-area hotels save transit time |
| Key reservation | Day 1 dinner, any kaiseki restaurant |
| Overall pace | Moderate — starts early, relaxed afternoons |

## Day 1: Eastern Kyoto

### Morning — Fushimi Inari (6:00–8:00am)
Start at Fushimi Inari Taisha before the crowds arrive. JR Nara Line from Kyoto Station to Inari (5 min, ¥150). Walk the lower torii tunnels, hike to Yotsutsuji junction for city views, return by 8am. See the [Fushimi Inari guide](/blog/sights/fushimi-inari).

### Late Morning — Kiyomizudera & Higashiyama (9:00am–1:00pm)
Bus or taxi to Kiyomizudera (¥500, opens 6am). The morning window 9–11am is ideal — tour groups arrive later. Budget 60–75 minutes inside.

Walk downhill through Sannenzaka and Ninenzaka — the stone-paved slopes with traditional facades that define old Kyoto's visual identity. Continue through Ishibei-koji lane and Kodaiji Temple to Maruyama Park and Yasaka Shrine. See the [Higashiyama walking guide](/blog/neighborhoods/higashiyama).

### Afternoon — Gion Exploration (2:00–5:00pm)
Lunch in the Gion area (obanzai sets recommended). Then walk Hanamikoji and Shimbashi — the streets at the heart of Kyoto's geisha culture. Quieter and more atmospheric in the afternoon when midday tour groups have cleared.

Optional: Nishiki Market, 15 minutes west.

### Evening — Dinner in Gion or Pontocho (7:00pm+)
Reserve in advance for anything mid-to-high range. The maiko-sighting window (5–7pm on weekdays near Hanamikoji) comes just before dinner — position yourself accordingly.

## Day 2: Western & Northern Kyoto

### Morning — Arashiyama (7:30am–12:00pm)
JR Saga-Arashiyama (17 min from Kyoto Station). Bamboo grove before 9am, then Tenryu-ji garden (¥500), Nonomiya Shrine, and Togetsukyo Bridge. If time allows: Jojakko-ji temple or Okochi Sanso villa.

See the [Arashiyama district guide](/blog/neighborhoods/arashiyama).

Late morning breakfast or early lunch in Arashiyama (tofu restaurants or cafes near Togetsukyo).

### Afternoon — Northern Kyoto: Kinkaku-ji + Ryoan-ji (1:30–5:00pm)
Bus from Arashiyama to Kinkaku-ji area (about 50 minutes). Kinkaku-ji (¥500) takes 30–40 minutes — a one-way garden circuit. Then walk 10 minutes to Ryoan-ji (¥600) for the famous 15-stone rock garden.

The combination of Kinkaku-ji and Ryoan-ji covers two of Japan's most famous garden compositions and takes a total of 2–2.5 hours.

See the [Kinkaku-ji guide](/blog/sights/kinkakuji).

### Evening — Dinner Near Nijo or Return to Gion
Nijo Castle area or return east for dinner. Central Kyoto has excellent mid-range dining along Kawaramachi and Teramachi streets.

## Day 3: Central Kyoto or Day Trip

### Option A: Central Kyoto

**Morning — Nijo Castle (9:00am)**
Take the Tozai subway line to Nijo-jo-mae. Nijo Castle (¥1,300) is one of Kyoto's most underrated sights — the nightingale floors, 800 Kano school paintings in Ninomaru Palace, and UNESCO garden deserve 2+ hours. See the [Nijo Castle guide](/blog/sights/nijo-castle).

**Late Morning — Philosopher's Path & Nanzenji (11:30am)**
The Philosopher's Path (*Tetsugaku-no-Michi*) is a 2km canal-side walk lined with cherry trees (spectacular in late March/early April). Walk north from Eikan-do to Ginkaku-ji (the Silver Pavilion, ¥500) or south from Ginkaku-ji to Nanzenji.

Nanzenji (free main grounds, ¥600 for Sanmon gate) has an aqueduct and several sub-temples.

**Afternoon** — Nishiki Market for lunch, then Kyoto Imperial Palace Park (free, vast) or final shopping in the Kawaramachi covered arcade.

### Option B: Nara Day Trip
45 minutes by Kintetsu Limited Express from Kyoto Station. See the [Nara Day Trip guide](/blog/day-trips/nara-from-kyoto). Recommended if temples and deer are priorities; add to Day 3 and keep Day 2 afternoon for Kinkaku-ji.
