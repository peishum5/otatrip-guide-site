# Where to Stay in Kyoto: Best Areas for First‑Timers

<!-- HERO_IMAGE: Neighborhood collage (Gion/Higashiyama/Downtown/Station). 2400x1350. -->

## Quick Picks

| Area | Why stay | Watch‑outs | Good for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gion/Higashiyama | Historic lanes, evening walks | Crowds, stairs | Couples, culture lovers |
| Downtown (Kawaramachi/Karasuma) | Best dining/transit | Busy/noisy streets | Foodies, shoppers |
| Kyoto Station | Easy transfers, value hotels | Less charm | Day‑trips, families |
| Arashiyama | Nature, riverside | Far from center | Slow trips, repeat visitors |

## Gion & Higashiyama

- Atmosphere: lanterns, wooden facades, narrow lanes.
- Hotel types: boutique ryokan, machiya rentals, small inns.
- Access: walkable to Kiyomizu/Yasaka; buses/taxis for elsewhere.
- Photo & noise etiquette applies day and night.

## Downtown (Kawaramachi/Karasuma)

- Transit: easy buses/subways; walk to Nishiki/Food streets.
- Hotels: modern business hotels, apartments, hostels.
- Nightlife and shopping.

## Kyoto Station Area

- Seamless arrivals/departures; luggage storage; large hotel inventory.
- Good base for Nara/Osaka day trips.

## Arashiyama (for longer stays)

- Peaceful mornings by the river; Tenryu‑ji gardens.
- Farther transit to central sights; plan clusters.

- Prioritize transit + evening atmosphere; minimize daily backtracking.
