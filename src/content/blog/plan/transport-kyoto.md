# How to Get Around Kyoto: Bus, Subway, IC Cards

<!-- HERO_IMAGE: Kyoto bus or subway platform. -->

## TL;DR

- Use IC cards (ICOCA/Suica/PASMO); avoid buying single tickets.
- Buses cover temples but crawl in traffic; combine with rail + walking.
- Taxis are efficient for 2–4 people over short hops.

## IC Cards

- Where to buy/top‑up: major stations, convenience stores.
- Using: tap in/out; some buses tap on boarding only.
- Lost card: purchase replacement; balances aren’t transferable across cards.

## Buses vs. Trains

| Situation | Best choice |
|---|---|
| Peak hours (8–10am, 4–7pm) | Trains or walking |
| Cross‑town (west↔east) | Taxi or rail + short taxi |
| Short intra‑area hops | Bus or walk |

## Sample Routes

- Kyoto Station → Fushimi Inari: JR Nara Line (Inari).
- Gion → Arashiyama: Keihan → Hankyu via Kawaramachi/Katsura; or taxi off‑peak.
- Downtown → Kiyomizu: bus 100/206 (crowded) or taxi for 2–4 people.

## Taxis & Ride‑Hailing

- Flag down at stands; cash/credit accepted; English support varies.
- Ride‑hailing apps: DiDi/GO in major areas.
- Etiquette: rear doors open automatically; seatbelts required.

## Biking & Walking

- Many sidewalks are narrow; walk single file in old districts.
- Bike rentals exist; park only in designated areas (strict fines).

## Accessibility

- Low‑floor buses common; stations have elevators; some temples have steep steps.
- See individual sight pages for stairs/ramps/toilet notes.
