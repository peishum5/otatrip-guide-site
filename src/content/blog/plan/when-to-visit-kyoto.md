# Best Time to Visit Kyoto (By Month)

<!-- HERO_IMAGE: Seasonal collage (sakura, green summer, autumn leaves, winter lights). -->

## Quick Answer

- Best overall: late March–early April (post‑peak sakura), late May, late Oct–mid Nov weekdays.
- Quiet value: January–February (cold, clear), early July (before school holidays).

## Seasons at a Glance

| Season | Weather | Crowds | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spring | Mild; cherry blossoms | Very high | Book early; early mornings essential |
| Summer | Hot/humid; showers | Medium | Gion Matsuri in July; AC breaks |
| Autumn | Cool/crisp; foliage | Very high | Peak colors ~mid‑Nov |
| Winter | Cold; dry | Low | Clear views; illuminations |

## Month‑by‑Month Highlights

<!-- CHART_PLACEHOLDER: Insert crowd/weather chart image with alt text. -->

- Jan–Feb: crisp skies, lowest hotel rates; illuminations continue.
- Mar: early blooms; late Mar can be peak sakura.
- Apr: peak then petals; post‑peak week is great.
- May: green leaves, comfortable temps; Golden Week is crowded.
- Jun: rainy season begins; hydrangeas; bring umbrella.
- Jul: hot; Gion Matsuri events mid‑July.
- Aug: very hot; Obon mid‑Aug; pace down and hydrate.
- Sep: typhoon risk; late month cools.
- Oct: pleasant; foliage starts late month.
- Nov: peak foliage; pre‑book everything.
- Dec: quiet after mid‑Dec; lights and clear views.

## Packing List

- Compact umbrella, layers, good walking shoes, power bank, refillable bottle.

## Where to Stay by Season

- Spring/Autumn: stay walkable to targets to avoid transit jams (Gion/Downtown).
- Summer/Winter: Kyoto Station value and AC access are handy.
